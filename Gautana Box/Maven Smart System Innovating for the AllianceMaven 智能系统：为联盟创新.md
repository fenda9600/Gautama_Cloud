## Exploiting the Power of AI Detections with Safran.AI 🇫🇷利用 AI 检测的力量 Safran.AI 🇫🇷

[Safran.AI](https://safran.ai/) develops AI solutions for aerospace and defense. Their pre-trained AI algorithms perform automatic detection, classification, and identification of objects of military interest, enabling analysts to leverage the large amount of satellite data they receive into accurate insights in a matter of minutes. NATO intelligence officers are often required to analyze hundreds of satellite images. For NATO’s MSS Industry Day, the teams explored how deploying [Safran.AI](https://safran.ai/) detectors into MSS can deliver a more efficient, data-driven process. The end result was a suite of tools to empower analysts to extract high-value signal from high volumes of data when monitoring specific sites and performing temporal comparisons of those sites. To begin, satellite data enriched with [Safran.AI](https://safran.ai/) detections were ingested into MSS. This data were then transformed into backing datasets that comprised the core [Safran.AI](https://safran.ai/) Ontology within MSS. The Ontology objects — like a Satellite Image object or Detection object — were the foundation of data-rich applications where analysts could conduct workflows. The Ontology also provided AIP Agents with the semantic, structured information they need to reason and act in the platform. The operational result of this technical integration work was a suite of AI-powered applications for imagery exploration and exploitation. In one tool, users could view all imagery associated with an Area of Interest, and for each image, conduct comprehensive analysis and activity monitoring. For example, when an analyst clicked into Port of Baltiysk and selected two different Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) images captured only a short time apart, they could dynamically apply a layer to highlight the differences between these images, quickly surfacing the movement of assets that may contribute to important indications and warnings. Such insights can allow analysts to respond quickly, such as requesting further satellite data collection. ## Tasking Drones at the Tactical Edge with Quantum Systems 🇩🇪与量子系统 🇩🇪 一起在战术边缘部署无人机

[Quantum Systems](https://quantum-systems.com/) is a German aerial data intelligence and drone company that develops and deploys multi-sensor data collection products to government agencies and commercial customers. With their commercial, dual-use approach — serving both commercial and defense customers — Quantum has the unusual advantage of applying commercial development speed to the defense industry. Through their MOSAIC Unmanned Systems (UXS) software, they deliver seamlessly integrated command and control of unmanned systems with AI-powered planning, real-time evaluation, and universal scalability for multi-domain operations. Modern battlefields require the ability to translate theater-level objectives into immediate drone tasking. This has been starkly demonstrated in Ukraine, where both Palantir and Quantum Systems have deployed their technologies. At NATO’s Industry Day, Quantum’s teams successfully integrated [Quantum’s MOSAIC UXS](https://quantum-systems.com/mosaic-uxs/) with Palantir MSS to connect strategic intent with tactical execution, generating mission plans in MSS, sending those plans to MOSAIC for execution, and then receiving key insights back in MSS. At the center of the integration was the “maneuver task” created in MSS and then sent to MOSAIC. The “maneuver” was an object in the Quantum Ontology and represented the area of interest for a drone mission, the type of detections that were of interest, and the mission priority. For example, a user monitoring the overall picture of the battlefield in MSS could create a new “maneuver task” and define armored vehicles and personnel as desired detections. By applying an Ontology Action on top of the “maneuver task” object, MSS was then able to execute dedicated logic using the Ontology. In this case, MSS sent out the tasking request to the external system, MOSAIC. (See the demo in Figure 3.)[Quantum Systems](https://quantum-systems.com/) 是一家德国空中数据情报和无人机公司，开发并部署多传感器数据收集产品，面向政府机构和商业客户。凭借其商业双用途方案——服务于商业和国防客户——Quantum 拥有将商业开发速度应用于国防工业的独特优势。通过 MOSAIC 无人系统（UXS）软件，他们实现了无人系统的无缝集成指挥与控制，结合 AI 驱动的规划、实时评估和多域作战的通用可扩展性。 现代战场需要将战区级目标转化为无人机的即时任务。这一点在乌克兰得到了鲜明的证明，Palantir 和 Quantum Systems 都部署了他们的技术。在北约工业日，Quantum 团队成功将 [Quantum 的 MOSAIC UXS](https://quantum-systems.com/mosaic-uxs/) 与 Palantir MSS 集成，将战略意图与战术执行相结合，在 MSS 中生成任务计划，发送给 MOSAIC 执行，最后在 MSS 中获得关键洞察。 整合的核心是 MSS 中创建并发送给 MOSAIC 的“机动任务”。“机动”是量子本体论中的一个对象，代表无人机任务的关注区域、感兴趣的探测类型以及任务优先级。例如，用户在 MSS 中监控战场整体情况，可以创建新的“机动任务”，并将装甲车辆和人员定义为期望的侦测对象。通过在“机动任务”对象上应用本体动作，MSS 便能够使用本体执行专用逻辑。在这种情况下，MSS 向外部系统 MOSAIC 发送任务请求。（见图 3 中的演示。）

## Creating Flexibility through Course of Action Generation with Hadean 🇬🇧通过 Hadean 🇬🇧 的行动方案生成创造灵活性

Hadean is a UK-based defense technology company that develops and fields next-generation software solutions. For NATO MSS Industry Day, its team integrated Hadean’s “[dominAI](https://hadean.com/dominai/)” tool with MSS. The result was a comprehensive, data-driven AI and simulation product suite that empowers users to achieve decision superiority in contested environments where adversaries adapt quickly and exploit delays. DominAI is an AI-powered planning tool that generates, simulates, and validates multiple Courses of Action (COAs) to identify the optimal COA based on risk, logistics, and doctrine. It does this by fusing live, historical, and synthetic data to help military commanders make faster, better-informed decisions across all operational levels. For the Industry Day event, MSS served as an authoritative data feed to power Course of Action development in dominAI, and ingested dominAI-developed COAs back into MSS to ensure commanders could act with all available information. MSS integrated with Hadean's dominAI via open APIs to create a powerful military planning and analysis system that leverages both the data and AI models hosted in MSS. As an example, live tracks from MSS were ingested directly into dominAI in real time. From there, a user could configure mission planning parameters, including force laydown, equipment data pulled from MSS, operational objectives, and relevant doctrine such as Allied Joint Publications. DominAI would then call LLMs hosted in MSS to generate unique and feasible COAs based on these comprehensive inputs. Once generated, these COAs could be visualized alongside synchronization matrices, with the ability to step through each sequence of a plan in detail. During COA simulation, dominAI's agentic solution ran parallelized simulations of each course of action simultaneously and performed a comparative analysis across all options.
